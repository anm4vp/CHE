Passive NOx Adsorber(PNA)

Project description

Passive NOx Adsorber (PNA) is a newly developed catalytic technology to reduce NOx emissions during engine “cold start”. High temperature (≥ 200 ℃) is needed to decompose urea and activate the selective catalytic reduction reaction. So until that temperature is reached, emissions escape. As a supplement to existing aftertreatment systems, PNAs trap NOx during cold start and subsequently releases the NOx when the temperature rises into the SCR effective operating window. With increasing engine fuel efficiencies and the “150 ℃ challenge” being proposed by DOE, cold start control becomes more important and emission control at lower temperature will become a bigger challenge, motivating us to understand how a PNA works and how to make it work better.
